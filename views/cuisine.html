Must-Try Dishes

Himachali Cuisine

Drawing inspiration from both Punjabi and Tibetan influences, the cuisine of Himachal Pradesh offers a delightful fusion. A typical meal includes dals, rice, hearty broths, flatbreads, and vegetable dishes, each infused with local aromatic spices and mountain herbs.

🫓
Bread

Sidu

A wheat-based steamed bread stuffed with poppy seeds or walnuts — a staple of Himachali households, especially in Kullu.

🥞
Breakfast

Patande

A unique local version of pancakes made from wheat flour, popular in the Kullu region as a morning staple.

🫘
Dal

Kali Dal

Black lentils slow-cooked with local spices — a hearty, nutritious staple dish found in every Himachali home.

🥔
Curry

Aloo Palda

Potatoes cooked in a yoghurt-based gravy with local spices — a comfort food classic across the state.

🎃
Vegetable

Auriya Kadoo

A delectable pumpkin preparation cooked with mustard seeds and local herbs — a seasonal favourite.

🥟
Snack

Momos

Tibetan-influenced steamed dumplings filled with vegetables or meat, ubiquitous across Himachal Pradesh.

🌿
Condiment

Chutney of Til

A sesame seed chutney with a nutty, tangy flavour — served as a condiment with most traditional meals.

🍲
Curry

Khatta

A tangy tamarind-based curry, often made with potatoes or bottle gourd — a signature Himachali flavour.

🌸
Delicacy

Spicy Lotus Stems

Lotus stems cooked in a spiced gravy — a unique and flavourful Himachali delicacy found in the Kangra region.

Culinary Influences

Himachali cuisine sits at the crossroads of Punjabi heartiness and Tibetan simplicity, shaped by altitude, climate, and centuries of cultural exchange along ancient trade routes.
